1. What is Phishing?

Phishing relies on social engineering, exploiting human psychology to steal information. The threat actor masquerades as a trusted entity (like a company) to trick the recipient into performing a desired action. Phishing messages often inject a sense of urgency (e.g., threatening account suspension) to compel users to act quickly without scrutiny.

2. What is Password Security & Protection?

A strong password must be long (at least 12 characters) and complex. It must also be unique to each account. The most vital supplemental measure is enabling Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) and using a password manager to securely generate and store complex credentials.

3. What is Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)?

MFA requires at least two distinct forms of proof before granting access, adding extra security layers beyond a single password. These factors are Knowledge, Possession, and Inherence. MFA is critical because it prevents an attacker from gaining access even if they steal your password.
